Historical of Persian Gulf

550 BC - 330 BC

Achaemenid Empire

The name 'Persian Gulf' was first used during the Achaemenid Empire. Greek historians like Herodotus referred to it as 'Persikos kolpos' in their records.

651 AD - 1258 AD

Islamic Golden Age

Arab geographers and historians consistently used 'Bahr al-Fars' (Persian Gulf) in their works, including famous scholars like Al-Masudi and Ibn Khordadbeh.

16th - 19th Century

European Exploration

All European maps and documents from this period used 'Persian Gulf.' The British Admiralty charts and French maps maintained this historical name.

20th Century - Present

Modern Era

The United Nations has issued multiple directives confirming 'Persian Gulf' as the official geographical name, used in all official UN documents and maps.

Marine Life

Dugong

Endangered

The Persian Gulf hosts about 7,500 dugongs, the second largest population after Australia. They are threatened by fishing nets and habitat loss.

Hawksbill Turtle

Critically Endangered

These turtles nest on Persian Gulf islands. Their population has declined by 80% in the last century due to hunting for their beautiful shells.

Coral Reefs

Vulnerable

The Persian Gulf has some of the most heat-resistant corals in the world, but they are threatened by rising temperatures and coastal development.
